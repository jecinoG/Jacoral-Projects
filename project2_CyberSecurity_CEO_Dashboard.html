Business Email Compromise (BEC)
Attackers impersonate your CEO, CFO, or a trusted supplier via email — tricking employees into transferring funds or sharing financial data. The #1 threat to Nigerian and Gulf businesses. Losses are rarely recovered.
Nigerian SME incidents 2024↑ 340%
🎣
Spear Phishing — Executive Targeting
Highly personalised attacks on CEOs and their assistants. Attackers research LinkedIn, social media, and company websites to craft convincing fake messages from known contacts, lawyers, or government bodies.
CEOs targeted globally per day400,000+
🔒
Ransomware — Business Lockout
Malware encrypts all your business files and demands payment in crypto to restore access. One wrong email click by any staff member can lock your entire operation. Average downtime: 22 days. Avg. ransom: $200,000+.
Africa ransomware rise (2024)↑ 185%
📱
WhatsApp & Social Media Fraud
Impersonation of your brand or personal account on WhatsApp to defraud your clients and contacts. In the Nigerian real estate market, fake "agents" clone profiles to steal deposits. Your clients trust your name — protect it.
WhatsApp fraud reports — Nigeria↑ 520%
🏦
Banking & Payment Interception
Attackers intercept payment communication between you and clients or suppliers — changing bank account details at the last moment. Extremely common in Nigerian and UAE property transactions. Called "Man-in-the-Middle" attacks.
Avg. loss per property deal$47,000
🔍
AI-Generated Deepfake Scams
New threat: AI clones the voice or video of a CEO to authorise fraudulent transactions. A UAE company lost $25M in 2024 to a deepfake video call impersonating their CFO. This threat is accelerating rapidly with AI tools becoming accessible.

Real Scams Targeting Nigerian & Dubai CEOs Right Now
Know these. Share them with your team. They cost businesses millions every month.
// BEC ATTACK
"Hi, it's me — the CEO. I'm in a meeting, can't talk. Please urgently transfer ₦5M to this new account for a confidential deal. Don't tell anyone yet."
⚠️ Always verify financial requests via a separate phone call to the real person — never by replying to the same email.
// PROPERTY FRAUD
"Dear buyer, please note our bank account details have changed effective immediately. Please send your property deposit to this new account: [fake account]."
⚠️ Legitimate developers never change payment accounts mid-transaction by email. Always confirm by calling a number you found independently.
// WHATSAPP CLONE
A contact receives a WhatsApp from a number pretending to be you, saying: "I'm in an emergency, can you send ₦200K? I'll return it tomorrow. Don't call — I'm in a meeting."
⚠️ Inform your network: the real you will NEVER ask for money via WhatsApp without a prior phone call. Establish this publicly on LinkedIn.
